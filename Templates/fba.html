Amazon FBA Business Components

Amazon FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon) is a business model where you sell products, and Amazon handles storage, packing, shipping, and customer service. Sellers send inventory to Amazon warehouses, allowing for faster shipping (Prime) and easier scaling. It is used for private label, wholesale, and arbitrage, but requires management of inventory fees, which increase for long-term storage.

  • How It Works:You list products on Amazon, ship inventory to Amazon fulfillment centers, and they handle logistics, customer service, and returns.

  • Benefits: Access to Amazon Prime customers, expert logistics, and automated customer service.

  • Loan term—the amount of time over which the loan must be repaid in full. Most fixed-rate mortgages are for 15, 20, or 30-year terms. A shorter period, such as 15 or 20 years, typically includes a lower interest rate.

  • Costs & Fees:FBA fees include storage, fulfillment, and potentially high fees for products stored longer than 181 days.

  • Strategies: Many sellers focus on private labeling (creating a brand), wholesale, or retail/online arbitrage to source products.

  • Challenges:
    • Managing inventory levels to avoid high storage fees, competitive marketplace, and capital constraints.

How Amazon FBA Works in Simple Terms

When you use Fulfillment by Amazon, you send your stock to Amazon warehouses in the UK. Amazon then takes care of:

  • Storing your products
  • Packing orders
  • Delivering to customers
  • Handling customer questions
  • Processing returns

This system saves time and helps you scale faster, but it comes with structured fees that must be understood clearly.

The Main Costs UK Sellers Must Factor In

To understand your true profit, you must include every cost involved.

Amazon Selling Fee: Amazon takes a percentage from each sale depending on your product category.

Fulfillment Fee:This is the cost for Amazon to pick, pack, and deliver your product to customers.

Storage Cost:You pay Amazon to store your products in their warehouse each month.

Product Cost: This is the amount you pay your supplier or manufacturer.

Shipping and Import Charges: If you import goods into the UK, you must include:

  • Freight charges
  • Customs charges
  • Import tax where applicable

VAT Considerations:VAT plays a major role for UK sellers. It can affect:

  • Your selling price
  • Your Amazon fees
  • Your overall profit margin

Getting this wrong can affect your entire business position.

Common Mistakes Many UK Sellers Make

Even experienced sellers can make costly errors.

  1. Not Including VAT Properly:VAT is often missed or underestimated, which leads to false profit expectations.
  2. Forgetting Hidden Costs:Small costs such as packaging, returns, or storage can reduce profit over time.
  3. Setting Prices Too Low:Trying to compete only on price can destroy your profit margin.
  4. Not Reviewing Figures Regularly:Costs change over time, so your calculations must be updated often.
